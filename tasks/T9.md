# T9: Multi-day log slicing at midnight

**Goal:** Populate `PlanResult.days` by slicing the flat `segments` timeline into
per-calendar-day `DayLog`s, splitting any segment that crosses midnight. This is the
keystone that makes the (already-built) T8 `DailyLogSheet` render real data.

## Design (agreed 2026-06-01)

- **Backend-only.** The DRF serializers already serialize `days`, and `TripPlanner`
  already renders a `DailyLogSheet` per day. Filling `days` is all that's missing —
  no frontend change.
- **Units to match T8:** each `DayLog` segment's `start_min`/`end_min` are
  **minute-of-day (0–1440)** within that day (T8 places them on a 00:00–24:00 grid).
- **Anchor by start time:** a segment at trip-minute `m` sits at absolute clock-minute
  `start_time_minutes + m`. Midnights are multiples of 1440; `date_offset = abs // 1440`.
- **Split at midnight:** a segment spanning a midnight becomes one piece per day,
  each with day-local times; `miles` apportioned by the piece's duration fraction.
- **Off-duty gaps are T8's job:** the engine timeline starts at the first segment
  (trip start), so day 0's pre-trip hours and the final day's tail are simply absent
  from `days` — `DailyLogSheet` fills unaccounted grid time as off-duty. (This is why
  a 7am start renders off-duty 00:00–07:00.)

  - `slice_days(segments, start_time_minutes) -> list[DayLog]`: convert each segment
    to absolute minutes, split at each 1440 boundary, emit day-local `DutySegment`s
    (minute-of-day times, apportioned miles), grouped into `DayLog`s ordered by
    `date_offset`.
  - Test: a within-one-day timeline yields one `DayLog` with shifted local times; a
    segment crossing midnight yields two `DayLog`s split at the boundary; total sliced
    duration equals the original timeline duration (conservation).

- [x] **T9.2** Wire into `plan_trip`
